My Journey

My name is Shamsurahman Jaihoon. I am 30 years old from Badakhshan of Afghanistan presently living in Tajikistan. I was born in a society where customs and traditions prevailed over law and individual rights were violated. Despite this, I always had a goal, to become a protector of human rights and bring justice to the vulnerable people in my society.
Henceforth, after graduating from school I pursued my Bachelor in Law and Political Science. Consequently, after graduation, I served in my profession for more than 6 years.
Initially, I started by registering myself as a licensed defense lawyer and helped many people with legal consultation advice. Throughout my professional life, I tried to be a supporter of those who were oppressed by powerful figures and this was the biggest mission I have. On the other hand, I realized that women are the most vulnerable part of our society, thus I started to provide full free legal services for women, defending their rights and as a result of this I was honored by the leadership of the Human Rights Commission as well.
Additionally, I allotted my time to volunteering for CTEO humanitarian organization (a local nonprofit organization in Afghanistan) serving the internally displaced people by launching campaigns and collecting aid for flood-affected families, and setting up educational, legal, and cultural seminars for university students. Living and loving my profession I learned how important is the existence of law in society and how meaningful it could be when it is at the service of all and include everyone.
Unluckily, the recent crisis has devasted my settled life in Afghanistan and I had to travel to Tajikistan. Keeping in mind my dreams even as a refugee I did not stop learning and serving. I completed the language and culture training programs that were designed for refugees. Recently I became a certified trainer by the American Councils and I voluntarily started teaching and conducting training for refugees in Tajikistan.
